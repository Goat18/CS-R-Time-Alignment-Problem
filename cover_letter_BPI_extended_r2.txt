> Reviewer #1: This paper presents an approach to align misaligned 
> timestamps, providing that the traces are already aligned on the 
> activities. The approach allows for so-called mixed moves, where a mixed 
> move is a combination of a stamp move and a delay move. A stamp move 
> only effects the timestamp of the current event, whereas the delay move 
> effects the current event and all succeeding events. The paper shows 
> that in two cases an alignment can be computed in linear (w.r.t. the 
> length of the trace) time:
> 1. If the costs of a stamp move are at least twice the costs of a delay 
> move. This is a bit trivial, as then every stamp move can be replaced by 
> two delay moves, hence we only need to consider delay moves.
> 2. If the costs of a stamp move are identical to the costs of a delay 
> move. This is more involved, and requires clever choosing between stamp 
> moves and delay moves.
> 
> The approach does not take any other perspectives into account. Like if 
> an event was performed by some resource (organizational perspective) and 
> that it is somehow clear that a resource could not have performed an 
> activity on a certain time, then the approach may still decide that this 
> is the best solution.
We agree with this comment. Indeed our setting does not take this kind of
situations into account. These things could be modeled with richer formalisms;
here we focus on the more uniform setting, which already poses interesting
questions.
We added a comment in the perspectives.
